Allen Newell

Founding Father of Artificial Intelligence

An interactive presentation on the man who taught machines how to think.

Early Life & A Legendary Partnership

  • Born in San Francisco in 1927. He studied physics at Stanford and mathematics at Princeton.
  • While working at the RAND Corporation, he met **Herbert A. Simon**, a political scientist.
  • This began one of the most famous collaborations in science. Together, they decided to build a "thinking machine."
  • They co-founded the AI lab at Carnegie Mellon University.

Major Contribution: The First AI Program

  • In 1956, Newell, Simon, and J.C. Shaw created the **Logic Theorist**.
  • It is widely considered the **first-ever AI program**.
  • It was designed to mimic human problem-solving skills to prove mathematical theorems.
  • It successfully proved 38 of the first 52 theorems in the famous math book *Principia Mathematica*.
How Logic Theorist Worked

The General Problem Solver

  • Their next big project was the **General Problem Solver (GPS)** in 1959.
  • GPS was a landmark attempt to create a single program that could solve many different kinds of problems, from logic puzzles to geometry.
  • It was based on their theory of how humans think, using goals and sub-goals.
  • This work established **Symbolic AI**, the idea that intelligence is the manipulation of symbols.

Influence on Modern-Day AI

The Blueprint for Reasoning

  • Newell and Simon won the **Turing Award** in 1975, computing's highest honor.
  • Their ideas about search, problem spaces, and modeling human cognition are still core concepts in AI.
  • His work on "cognitive architectures" like Soar influenced the design of AI agents in robotics and games.
  • He helped establish AI as a legitimate field of scientific inquiry.
