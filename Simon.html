Herbert A. Simon

Pioneer of AI & Human Decision-Making

An interactive presentation on the Nobel Prize winner who helped define artificial intelligence.

Early Life & A Polymath's Mind

  • Born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, in 1916. He studied political science at the University of Chicago.
  • He wasn't just a computer scientist; he was also a renowned psychologist and economist.
  • He met **Allen Newell** at the RAND Corporation, forming a legendary partnership to study how humans and machines solve problems.
Watch: Simon on Problem Solving

Major Contribution: The Logic Theorist

  • In 1956, with Allen Newell and J.C. Shaw, he co-created the **Logic Theorist**.
  • This program is considered the **first true AI program** ever written.
  • It was designed to find proofs for mathematical theorems, mimicking the steps of a human mathematician.
  • This showed that computers could reason with symbols, not just numbers.

Bounded Rationality & Problem Solving

  • Simon introduced the idea of **"bounded rationality."**
  • He argued that humans don't make perfect decisions, but instead "satisfice"â€”they make choices that are "good enough" given limited time and information.
  • This was key to his AI work. He built machines that solved problems the way people do, using heuristics (mental shortcuts).
  • This led to the **General Problem Solver (GPS)**.

Influence on Modern-Day AI

A Lasting Legacy

  • In 1978, Simon won the **Nobel Prize in Economics** for his work on decision-making.
  • He also won the **Turing Award** in 1975 with Allen Newell.
  • His focus on modeling human cognition is the foundation of **cognitive science**.
  • Using heuristics is central to many modern AI algorithms, especially in search and planning.
