John McCarthy

The Man Who Named "Artificial Intelligence"

An interactive presentation on the visionary who defined a new field of science.

Early Life & Academic Brilliance

  • Born in Boston, Massachusetts, in 1927.
  • He was a brilliant mathematician, earning his Ph.D. from Princeton in 1951.
  • He taught at Stanford, Dartmouth, and MIT, where he became a leading figure in computer science.
Watch: McCarthy's Early Vision

Major Contribution: Naming a Field

  • In 1955, he wrote a proposal for a summer workshop to explore "thinking machines."
  • He needed a name for this new field. He chose **"Artificial Intelligence."**
  • The **1956 Dartmouth Workshop**, which he organized, is considered the official birth event of AI. It brought together all the key founding figures.
The Story of the Dartmouth Workshop

Inventing LISP: The Language of AI

  • In 1958, he invented the **LISP** (List Processing) programming language.
  • It was the second high-level programming language ever created (after Fortran).
  • Its ability to process symbols and lists, rather than just numbers, made it the perfect language for AI research.
  • LISP dominated the field of AI for over 30 years.
An Introduction to LISP

Influence on Modern-Day AI

A Lasting Legacy

  • He won the **Turing Award** in 1971 for his contributions.
  • LISP's "functional programming" style influences many modern languages, including Python and JavaScript.
  • He also pioneered the idea of **time-sharing**, which allowed many people to use one central computer at the same time. This is the foundation of cloud computing.
