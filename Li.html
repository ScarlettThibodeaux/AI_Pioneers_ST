Fei-Fei Li

Visionary of Modern AI & Creator of ImageNet

An interactive presentation on the woman who gave AI the power to see.

Early Life & A Journey in Science

  • Born in Beijing, China, in 1976. She moved to the U.S. at age 16.
  • She excelled in science and studied physics at Princeton University.
  • She earned her Ph.D. from Caltech, focusing on neuroscience and computer science.
Watch: Fei-Fei Li's American Dream

Major Contribution: ImageNet

  • In 2007, she began work on **ImageNet**, a revolutionary idea at the time.
  • It is a massive, free database of over 14 million images, all hand-labeled into thousands of categories.
  • Her goal was to provide enough high-quality data to "train" AI algorithms to recognize objects in pictures, just like a child learns.
  • This project solved the data scarcity problem that had held back computer vision for years.
Watch Her Explain ImageNet

Igniting the Deep Learning Revolution

  • ImageNet led to a yearly competition to see which AI could best identify objects.
  • In 2012, a "deep learning" model called AlexNet achieved a massive leap in accuracy, shocking the AI world.
  • This moment is often called the **"big bang" of modern AI**, proving that with enough data (from ImageNet), deep learning worked.
  • It kicked off the modern AI revolution we see today.

Influence on Modern-Day AI

Human-Centered AI

  • ImageNet's success enabled modern computer vision, which is used in everything from self-driving cars to medical diagnosis.
  • Today, she is a leading advocate for **"Human-Centered AI."**
  • She co-directs the Stanford Human-Centered AI Institute (HAI), which focuses on ethics, diversity, and ensuring AI benefits humanity.
