Background & Google Brain

  • Vaswani received his PhD from the University of Southern California (USC).
  • He joined **Google Brain**, a deep learning research team at Google.
  • There, he and his colleagues sought a better way to handle sequential data, like language, without the limitations of older models.
Watch: Vaswani on his Journey

Major Contribution: The Transformer

  • In 2017, Vaswani was the lead author of the paper **"Attention Is All You Need."**
  • It introduced the **Transformer**, a new network architecture based solely on a mechanism called **self-attention**.
  • This design could process entire sequences of data at once, making it much faster and more powerful than previous models (like LSTMs).
The Illustrated Transformer

The Birth of Modern LLMs

  • The Transformer architecture is the foundational technology for almost all modern **Large Language Models (LLMs)**.
  • Models like OpenAI's **GPT** series and Google's **BERT** are built directly on the Transformer design.
  • This invention is the key reason AI has seen such explosive growth in natural language understanding and generation.

Influence on Modern-Day AI

The Post-Transformer Era

  • Vaswani's work created a paradigm shift, making parallel processing of language possible and leading to today's era of massive AI models.
  • He has since left Google and co-founded **Adept AI Labs**, a company focused on building general intelligence.
  • The Transformer is now used in many areas beyond text, including computer vision and drug discovery.
